“Be careful what you wish for, there’s always a catch”. –

These words by Laurie Halse Anderson aptly apply to Indian tax payers today. The Budget 2020 saw the finance minister Nirmala Sitharaman announce a new tax regime with more tax slabs and lower tax rates. This was long demanded by most taxpayers, but it came with the catch of removal of all the deductions and exemptions that were available under the old tax regime. To add to this confusion, the finance minister gave taxpayers a choice between the new regime and existing one, leaving it to them to decide which they would like to opt for. All these factors acting together, instead of tax laws getting simpler, they are now more complex.
